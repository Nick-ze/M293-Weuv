Mastering the Volleyball Serve

The serve is the first attack in volleyball and a critical skill for every player. A well-executed serve can win points directly or put your opponent on the back foot.

Types of Serves

  • Underhand Serve: Great for beginners. Easier to control and get over the net.
  • Overhand Serve: More powerful and versatile, suitable for intermediate and advanced players.
  • Jump Serve: Advanced serve that adds power and speed. Great for aggressive play.
  • Float Serve: Has no spin, making it unpredictable and harder to pass.

How to Do a Jump Serve

  1. Start Position: Begin several steps behind the baseline with the ball in your non-dominant hand.
  2. Approach: Use a 3-step or 4-step approach, building momentum as you prepare to jump.
  3. Toss: Toss the ball high—about 4 to 6 meters in the air and slightly in front of you.
  4. Jump & Swing: Time your jump to meet the ball at its peak. Swing with full extension, hitting with the palm or heel of your hand.
  5. Follow Through: Land inside the court and be ready to transition into defense.

Tip:

The toss is everything so train it multiple times. If it’s too short or too far, don’t be afraid to let it drop and reset. A consistent toss leads to a powerful and accurate jump serve.
