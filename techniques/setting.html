Setting the Ball

The setter is the playmaker. A good set gives hitters the perfect opportunity to attack.

Setting Essentials

  • Hand Position: Form a triangle above your forehead with your thumbs and index fingers.
  • Soft Touch: Use your fingertips—not your palms—to gently push the ball.
  • Body Control: Set from your legs and core, not just your arms.

How to Set Correctly

  1. Ready Hands: Position your hands above your head early.
  2. Feet & Balance: Stay on the balls of your feet with knees slightly bent.
  3. Contact & Push: Contact the ball with soft fingertips and push up and out.

Tip:

Quiet hands and steady posture make for a clean set. Don’t rush—get under the ball and deliver it like a gift to your hitter.
